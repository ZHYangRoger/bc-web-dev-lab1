Mental Disorder and the Criminal-justice System

Issue Discussed

The cause and mechanism of a psychiatric disease , also known as a
mental disorder, is far more complex than what most people think.
The case for each specific patient, moreover, is also highly disparate
from one another. It is even a mystery that whether a psychiatric
professional can really examine if one is faking to be mentally ill
while in fact he is healthy. How does such a highly underdeveloped
area of study involved in the criminal-justice system of the United
States? Should people who are mentally ill be exculpated? Even so,
are psychiatric criminals really treated this way nowadays?
